NYC Water Stories

The map shows streams that criscrossed Manhattan in the year 1609 along with the shoreline at the time. Though most creeks and streams are no longer visible on the surface, many continue to course through subway tunnels, basements, and deep under streets and buildings.

Toggle the layers for the 100 year and 500 year flood maps developed by FEMA to see how much the original geography of the island impacts where flood hazards are expected today.
